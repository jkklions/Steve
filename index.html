In Memoriam

Steve Kiker

April 3, 1968  —  November 19, 2022

Devoted husband. Father of five. Sideline coach. Fisherman. Guitar player. The kind of man Blanchard won't forget.

Stephen Glenn Kiker

"He loved anything outdoors — especially fishing and hunting. He taught himself how to play guitar and had a good time giving concerts in the front yard."

Stephen Glenn Kiker, 54, of Blanchard, Oklahoma passed away on Saturday, November 19, 2022. Born on April 3, 1968 in Holdenville, Oklahoma, to James Monroe Kiker and Patricia Ann Secrest, Steve came to Blanchard in the fifth grade and never really left.

A 1986 Blanchard High School graduate, he married the love of his life, Julie Ka Knippelmier, on May 8, 1994 — and built a life around her and the family they made together.

Early Years

The Father

Always the sideline coach

Steve's world revolved around his kids — Stephanie, Holly, Stephen, Colten, and Jack. He was at every game, every practice, every ordinary Saturday morning that becomes the memory you carry for the rest of your life.

He coached from the sidelines even when he wasn't officially the coach. He cheered loud, drove far, and never missed a moment that mattered.

Blanchard, Oklahoma

While in high school Steve was very active in athletics. Later he enjoyed coaching baseball, watching Jack and Colten play Blanchard Lion basketball and baseball, and Holly cheer. He was always the sideline coach — that was his nature. Show up, support, never ask for the credit.

He loved anything outdoors. Fishing and hunting weren't hobbies, they were a state of mind. He knew every bend of the water, every quiet hour when the catfish would bite. The pond was his sanctuary.

Senior Night — Blanchard Baseball

The Guitar

Concerts in the front yard

Steve taught himself to play guitar. Nobody asked him to — that was just how he operated. He found something he loved, he worked at it, and then he shared it.

His neighbors will tell you they could hear him outside. They didn't mind. Nobody ever minded when it was Steve. He had a way of making the ordinary feel like something worth paying attention to.

Adventure

He was always up for it. Amusement parks, road trips, a Saturday drive with no particular destination — Steve made every outing feel like an event. His kids will tell you the best memories weren't planned. They just happened, because he was there.

The Friend

Steve was the kind of friend people talk about for the rest of their lives. Generous. Funny. Unfailingly real. There was no pretense — just Steve, exactly as he was. His work ethic was something people noticed young and never forgot. His sense of humor made any room better.

He married Julie on May 8, 1994, and spent 28 years building something with her that their children and grandchildren will carry forward. Boston Abbott — his first grandson — was one of the last great joys.

He is preceded in death by his mother, Patricia Ann Secrest. He will be greatly missed by his wife, his children, and anyone who knew him — which is to say, by a lot of people in Blanchard and beyond.
