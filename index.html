In Loving Memory

Steve

April 3, 1968 — November 19, 2022

Husband. Father of five. Coach, fisherman, guitar player — and the best man most of us ever knew.

His Story

He was just a really good man.

Steve grew up in Blanchard, Oklahoma and never really left — because everything he loved was right there. He married Julie on May 8, 1994, and spent 28 years building a life that most people only dream about.

Five kids — Stephanie, Holly, Stephen, Colten, and Jack. One grandson, Boston. A house that was never quiet and always full of love, sports on TV, and somebody needing something from Dad.

He coached baseball. He showed up to every game whether he was officially coaching or not. He taught himself guitar and played front yard concerts just because it made him happy. Whenever he got the chance he was outside — rod in hand at the pond, hunting before sunrise, breathing fresh Oklahoma air.

"He was always the sideline coach — even when he wasn't officially one. That was just who he was."

From the people who loved him

Steve passed away on November 19, 2022. He was 54 years old — way too young, way too soon. This site is for him. Not a museum. Just a place to tell his story.

❤️ One last thing

Please go get your heart checked.

Steve was 54. He was active. He was happy. He had absolutely no idea anything was wrong.

Heart disease doesn't give warnings. It doesn't care how good of a man you are or how many people still need you around.

Know your numbers. Get checked. Tell the people you love that you love them — today, not later.

That's what Steve would have wanted you to take from all of this.
